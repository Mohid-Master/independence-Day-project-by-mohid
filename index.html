A Glimpse into Pakistan's History

1947

Independence of Pakistan

On August 14, 1947, Pakistan was founded as an independent nation, marking the end of British rule in the subcontinent.

1956

First Constitution of Pakistan

The first constitution of Pakistan was adopted in 1956, establishing the country as the Islamic Republic of Pakistan.

1971

Separation of East Pakistan

In 1971, East Pakistan became Bangladesh after a brutal war of independence.

1988

First Female Prime Minister

Benazir Bhutto became the first woman to lead a Muslim-majority country as Prime Minister of Pakistan in 1988.

1998

Nuclear Tests

Pakistan conducted its first successful nuclear tests in 1998, becoming the seventh nuclear power in the world.

2011

Death of Osama Bin Laden

On May 2, 2011, Osama Bin Laden, the mastermind behind the 9/11 attacks, was killed in Abbottabad, Pakistan by U.S. Navy SEALs.

2018

Imran Khan Elected Prime Minister

In 2018, Imran Khan, a former cricket star, was elected as the Prime Minister of Pakistan, leading the PTI party to victory.

2022

Political Crisis and Imran Khan's Ouster

In April 2022, Imran Khan was removed from office following a no-confidence motion, leading to a significant political upheaval in the country.

2024

General Elections 2024

Pakistan held its general elections in 2024, marking a critical moment for the country's democratic processes and future governance.
